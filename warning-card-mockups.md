# Mobile Warning Card Mockups

These are text-based mockups for the proposed mobile microlearning warning cards. They can later be converted into Figma screens or a simple mobile/web prototype.

## Card 1: High-risk login link

**Warning:** High-risk link detected

This link appears to request login information, but the domain does not clearly match the expected organisation.

**Check before clicking:**

- Is the domain exactly correct?
- Were you expecting this message?
- Is it asking for your password or account reset?

**Recommended action:** Do not enter personal details until you verify the source.

## Card 2: Urgent account update message

**Warning:** Urgent wording detected

Phishing messages often use words such as urgent, verify, suspended, update or claim to pressure users into clicking quickly.

**Check before clicking:**

- Is the message trying to make you panic?
- Is there a deadline or threat?
- Can you contact the organisation through its official website instead?

**Recommended action:** Open the official website manually rather than clicking the link.

## Card 3: Suspicious domain structure

**Warning:** Suspicious domain pattern

This link contains extra words or subdomains that may imitate a trusted service.

**Example risk cue:**

A fake link may include a trusted brand name but still belong to a different domain.

**Recommended action:** Check the main domain carefully before continuing.

## Card 4: Learning summary

**What you learned:**

A link may be suspicious if it:

- uses urgent language;
- asks for passwords or account verification;
- contains unusual domain names;
- imitates a university, bank, job portal or email provider;
- comes from an unexpected sender.

**Safe habit:** When unsure, do not click. Go directly to the official website or app.
