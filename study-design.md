# Proposed Quantitative Study Design

## Study type

Quantitative pre-test/post-test study with a human-centred cybersecurity focus.

## Target participants

University students or early-career technology users who regularly use mobile phones for email, messaging, social media, banking, job applications or learning platforms.

## Independent variable

Exposure to mobile microlearning phishing warning cards.

## Dependent variables

- phishing awareness score;
- correct identification of phishing and legitimate links;
- confidence in identifying suspicious links;
- perceived usefulness of warning cards;
- perceived clarity of warning messages.

## Possible procedure

1. Participants complete a short consent and demographic section.
2. Participants complete a pre-test containing legitimate and suspicious mobile-link examples.
3. Participants view microlearning warning cards that explain common phishing cues.
4. Participants complete a post-test using a different set of links.
5. Participants rate the clarity, usefulness and trustworthiness of the warnings.

## Example statistical analysis

- Descriptive statistics: mean, standard deviation, percentages.
- Paired-sample t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test for pre-test vs post-test differences.
- Correlation or regression analysis to explore whether confidence, technology use or prior awareness predicts phishing detection performance.
- Reliability testing for awareness or perception scales if multiple items are used.

## Ethical considerations

The study would use simulated phishing examples or publicly available benign examples. No real phishing attack would be sent to participants. Participants would be informed that the study is about cybersecurity awareness. Personal data would be minimised and anonymised according to university ethics requirements.

## Feasibility statement

This study can be completed with standard survey tools, controlled link examples, mobile-warning mockups and statistical analysis software. It is suitable for a Master by Research project because it has a clear scope, measurable outcomes and practical cybersecurity relevance.
